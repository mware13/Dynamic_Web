NET NEUTRALITY

Protecting internet freedom

and the open net

WHAT IS
NET NEUTRALITY?

Net neutrality has been an intense topic of debate in recent years because of its potential impact on everyday life. Some confusion has surfaced as to what it really is, due to not having one technical definition. Put simply, net neutrality is the concept that Internet Service Providers (ISPs) must treat all internet traffic on an equal basis, no matter it's type or origin, content, or means used to transmit. Without net neutrality, ISPs have the ability to censor, regulate, and charge for internet traffic how they see fit.

As it currently stands, government regulation in North America has mostly kept net neutrality protected, allowing the internet to maintain its free nature. However, efforts are regularly made to eliminate net neutrality and put complete control of the internet into the hands of ISPs. It's important for us, the users, to fight against these efforts, and preserve internet freedom through net neutrality.

NET NEUTRALITY
FOR THE USER

The open net is required for individual expression and intellectual freedom, and preserving net neutrality would ensure that the internet remains free, open, and equal for anyone who wishes to engage in it. A free and open internet drives innovation, creativity, and entrepreneurship, promoting freedom of speech and the spread of knowledge and information. Net neutrality protects internet freedom by ensuring the control of the internet remains in the hands of the content providers, creators, and users as well as preventing unfair pricing practices and stimulating ISP competition to benefit the consumer.

NET NEUTRALITY
FOR THE ISPs

If the ISPs get their way, the abolishment of net neutrality would result in deals being made to give web services priority in reaching users. Fast and slow internet lanes would be created and content providers would be charged for bandwidth speed and use, resulting in web content not being accessed at equal speeds. In other words, ISPs can throttle the speed of delivery to privilege certain information over others, effectively slowing down some web content and services while speeding up others. The free and open net would take an enormous hit, inhibiting the spread of knowledge and information.

WHAT HAPPENS NOW?

While net neutrality remains intact in North America, efforts are regularly made by ISPs to destroy or work around current net neutrality laws and regulations. These corporations hold a lot of power but it is important to remember that there are far more of us than there are of them. Awareness is important; we, the content creators and users, must continue to make our voice heard and spread the word of net neutrality and its importance in preserving the open net.
